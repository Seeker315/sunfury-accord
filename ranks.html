The Codex

Ranks & Structure

Three branches. One premise. No branch outranks the others.

The Accord is not a hierarchy built on titles. It is a structure built on function and willpower. Each branch serves a purpose. No branch outranks the others.

There is no single leader, no single point of failure. A constellation of three capable individuals, equal to each other, each carrying responsibility over their respective domain. They are known as the Triad.

II. Process

Initiation & Advancement

I
Application
The applicant introduces themselves and their character. A brief conversation with the Triad or a designated member follows. An invitation as Adherent is extended or not. No open mass recruitment.
II
The Adherent Phase
The Adherent prepares a Trial of their own choosing, as long as it connects thematically to the Accord's premise. A magical theory. A tactical thesis. A network concept. The standard is intention, not quality. After four weeks without contact, a conversation is initiated. After six weeks without result, the Adherent phase ends without drama.
III
Trial of the Sun
The member announces their presentation to the Triad. The guild assembles. The presentation is held and the Accord formally admits the member with the appropriate entry rank. This moment is deliberately public. It is the first social anchor of membership.
IV
The Tempering Phase
The newly admitted member enters a period of deepening. No trial to complete, no deadline to meet. Through engagement, study and contribution, they develop the breadth the Accord expects of its proven members. When they are ready, they will know.
V
Ascension
Through participation, cunning and displays of willpower, those who wish to advance may approach the Triad themselves. A second trial will follow, a demonstration of depth and development.
