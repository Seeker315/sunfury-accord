I. Founding Manifest

We have been here before.

The loss of the Sunwell was not a tragedy born of power. It was a tragedy born of those entrusted with it.

For millennia, the Highborne and their heirs shaped the world around them. Not through prayer. Not through submission to forces greater than themselves. Through mastery. Power did not fail us. Those we entrusted with it did.

The Sunprince understood the problem with perfect clarity. He saw the dependency for what it was, and sought alternatives with the determination that defined our people at their finest. Yet where his will should have commanded the power, the power set the direction — and he became what it made of him.

Those who looked upon that fall and concluded the fault lay in ambition rather than in the loss of control drew the lesson of the frightened. They traded one dependency for another and called it wisdom. Piety is not a correction. It is a different form of surrender.

These forces — arcane, void, fel, light — are not masters to be served.
They are currents to be understood, and tools to be wielded.

The nature of power is secondary to the mastery of it. Mastery has always been the birthright of Highborne descent, expressed through the one thing no enemy has ever been able to strip from us: willpower.

The Sunfury Accord was founded on a single premise. Every decision we do not make, someone else will make for us. Every power we do not wield, someone else will wield against us.

II. Who We Are

Not an Order. Not a Brotherhood.

The Accord is a gathering of individuals who share a single premise. We are united by the refusal to let either define what we become.

We are not united by faith or by grief. Quel'Thalas has bled for its dependencies. The Accord exists to end them.

Those who join and preach submission to any cosmic force have fundamentally misunderstood what they signed up for. We dissect these forces. We do not kneel to them.

Mastery
The inheritance of the Highborne is not piety. It is the capacity for dominion over forces others fear to approach.
Willpower
The one thing no enemy has ever stripped from us. The Accord's ideology is built on self-determination, not surrender.
Knowledge
Arcane, void, fel, light — the nature of power is secondary to the mastery of it. We study what others refuse to touch.

III. Event Rhythm

The Monthly Cycle

Week One
Seeker Focus
Magical investigation, arcane theory, anomaly and research. The Seekers lead.
Week Two
Vanguard Focus
Field mission, active threat, confrontation, defence of Quel'Thalas.
Week Three
Cipher Focus
Intelligence, contacts, intrigue, covert operation. The unseen architecture.
Week Four
Accord Focus
All three branches converge. Council, major decisions, shared experience.

The branch defines the focus and the leading role within the event, not the guest list. All members are welcome at all events. Between main events, open RP threads and hooks keep the Accord alive without requiring leadership to be active every evening.

VI. Recruitment

Join the Accord

The Accord does not recruit in open calls. Applicants introduce themselves. A brief conversation follows. We decide whether to extend an invitation as Adherent.

Full membership requires a Trial, chosen by the applicant themselves, as long as it connects to the Accord's premise. A theory. A thesis. A network concept. The standard is intention, not quality.

The presentation is public. It is the first social anchor of membership. What you contribute to the Accord is what defines your place in it.

Sin'dorei and Shal'dorei are the natural core. Other races are welcome if they can genuinely operate within the Accord's premise.
Submission to any cosmic force — Light, Void, Fel or Arcane — as a matter of faith rather than mastery is incompatible with membership.
Advancement is self-initiated. Those who wish to progress approach the Triad. There is no automatic promotion.
No force is forbidden from study. What matters is who holds the leash.
